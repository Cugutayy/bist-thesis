Key Findings

What We Discovered

Five interconnected results revealing the dynamics of Turkey's IPO boom: massive underpricing, behavioural determinants, no aggregate herding, regulatory impact, and short-term overreaction.

📈 67.8% Mean Underpricing

BIST IPOs are significantly underpriced (t = 10.82, p < 0.001) when measured using consecutive limit-up methodology. The standard first-day return of 6.6% understates true underpricing by 10x. Median: 33.1%, with streaks up to 21 consecutive limit-up days.

📊 P/B Drives Underpricing

Cross-sectional OLS reveals price-to-book ratio is the only significant determinant (p = 0.040). P/E ratio is insignificant. Observable fundamentals explain only 10–21% of variation—underpricing is primarily behavioural, not fundamental.

🔍 No Aggregate Herding

CSAD herding test yields γ2 = 0.645 (p = 0.666)—no herding detected. However, CSAD test power averages only 59.37% (Zhang, 2024), so the null result should be interpreted with caution.

⚠ −7.4% Penalty CAAR

SPK manipulation penalties produce significant negative abnormal returns. Pre-event CAAR of −5.4% suggests market anticipation. 78.7% of cases are transaction-based manipulation. First formal event study of SPK penalties in Turkish literature.

💰 Short-Term SELL Signal

Contrarian strategy: −3.3% at 5 days (p = 0.021) after limit-up streak ends, consistent with overreaction. Only 32.8% of stocks positive. Long-term: +72.6% at 365 days (bull market effect).

💸 Inflation Erodes Returns

Cumulative CPI inflation exceeded 500% (2020–2025). BIST-100 rose 688% nominally but only ~40% in real terms. Median 365-day real IPO returns fall to roughly half their nominal values.

Methodology

How We Did It

Following Loughran, Ritter, and Rydqvist (1994, updated 2026) for price-limit markets, we measure initial returns at the first unconstrained closing price. Our approach combines four established methodologies.

🎯

Consecutive Limit-Up

Underpricingi = Pfree / Pclose − 1, where Pfree is the first unconstrained close. Turkish term: tavan serisi.

📐

Cross-Sectional OLS

4 progressive models with HC3 robust standard errors. Variables: ln(offer price), ln(market cap), P/E, P/B, year dummies, hot market.

📉

CSAD Herding (CCK 2000)

CSADt = α + γ1|Rm| + γ2Rm². Herding if γ2 < 0. Newey-West HAC standard errors.

📋

Event Study (MacKinlay 1997)

Market model with [-250, -31] estimation window. Boehmer et al. (1991) standardised test for CAR significance.
